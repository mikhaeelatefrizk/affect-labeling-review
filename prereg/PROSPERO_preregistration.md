## 6. Review questions

**Primary.** What is the magnitude, direction, and consistency of the effect of affect labeling on (a) amygdala activation, (b) ventrolateral and medial prefrontal cortex activation, (c) peripheral psychophysiological outcomes (skin conductance, heart rate, heart-rate variability, late positive potential), and (d) self-reported emotional experience, relative to active or passive control conditions?

**Secondary.** How do the effects vary by stimulus valence (negative vs. positive), stimulus intensity, language of labeling (native vs. foreign), age, clinical status, label specificity, and sequencing with subsequent regulation strategies?

## 9. Condition or domain being studied

Affect labeling as an emotion regulation strategy. The construct is defined as the act of producing or selecting a verbal label for an affective stimulus or felt state, with subsequent measurement of neural, psychophysiological, or behavioral outcomes. Adjacent constructs (cognitive reappraisal, expressive writing, emotional granularity, alexithymia) will be discussed in narrative synthesis but not pooled with affect labeling effect sizes.

## 10. Participants/population

**Inclusion.** Adults and adolescents (≥ 10 years) of any sex and any clinical status; both healthy participants and patient populations with anxiety, mood, personality, eating, substance-use, or trauma-related disorders.

**Exclusion.** Children under 10 years (developmental literature discussed narratively but not synthesized quantitatively due to age-graded measurement validity issues); non-human animals; clinical populations whose primary diagnosis is severe neurodegenerative or neurological disease.

## 11. Intervention(s) / exposure(s)

The exposure is affect labeling, operationalized as any of: forced choice from a list of emotion words; free generation of a verbal label; verbal naming of one's own current felt emotion; verbal labeling of a depicted facial expression or scene; instructed labeling during exposure or emotion induction. We will pre-specify whether each included study used "objective" (stimulus-content) versus "subjective" (own-emotion) labeling, since these have been shown to produce dissociable effects (McRae, Taitano, & Lane, 2010).

## 12. Comparator(s) / control

Passive viewing/observe; perceptual matching (gender, shape); cognitive reappraisal; distraction; acceptance/mindful observation; exposure-only; within-subject pre-test (for within-subjects designs).

## 13. Types of study

**Inclusion.** Randomized controlled trials, non-randomized controlled trials with a defined comparator, within-subjects experimental designs with active comparison conditions, coordinate-based or image-based neuroimaging meta-analyses (synthesized narratively only), and pre-registered preprints with peer-reviewed methodology equivalents.

**Exclusion.** Single-case clinical reports; editorial and theoretical papers without primary data; lay-press articles; studies whose only manipulation is expressive writing without a discrete labeling component.

## 14. Context

Both laboratory (standardized affective stimuli) and clinical (in vivo or imaginal exposure) contexts. Naturalistic / ambulatory studies discussed narratively.

## 15. Main outcome (meta-analytic)

Standardized mean difference (Cohen's d, converted to Hedges' g) for peripheral psychophysiological response (skin conductance, heart rate, heart-rate variability, late positive potential, fear-potentiated startle) comparing affect labeling to control conditions. Negative g indicates affect labeling reduced arousal.

## 16. Secondary outcomes (narrative)

Amygdala BOLD signal change (synthesized via existing coordinate-based meta-analyses); ventrolateral and medial prefrontal cortex BOLD signal change; self-reported emotional intensity, valence, or distress; behavioral approach (in exposure paradigms); clinical symptom change at follow-up.

## 17. Timing of outcome measurement

Both immediate post-manipulation and delayed (≥ 1 day) measurements will be extracted. Where multiple time points are reported, the longest delay will be used in the primary synthesis with sensitivity analysis at the immediate time point.

## 18. Additional moderators

Effect-size moderation by language of labeling (native vs. foreign), stimulus valence and intensity, population (healthy vs. clinical), and sequencing (labeling alone vs. labeling-then-reappraisal).

## 19. Selection process

**Pre-registered protocol (intent).** Two reviewers were to independently screen titles, abstracts, and full texts. Disagreements were to be resolved by discussion or, if unresolved, by a third reviewer. Inter-rater agreement was to be reported as Cohen's κ, with reviewers calibrating on 50 records and κ ≥ 0.70 required to proceed.

**Actual execution (deviation).** Screening was conducted by a single reviewer (the named contact in Section 5). Inter-rater agreement statistics are therefore **not available**. Aggregate PRISMA counts at each stage are canonical and machine-readable in [`prisma/prisma_counts.csv`](https://github.com/mikhaeelatefrizk/affect-labeling-review/blob/main/prisma/prisma_counts.csv). For ML researchers and reviewers re-using the corpus, a derived screening log under positive-unlabeled framing is published at [`data/screening/derived_screening_log.csv`](https://github.com/mikhaeelatefrizk/affect-labeling-review/blob/main/data/screening/derived_screening_log.csv). ## 20. Extraction template

The data-extraction schema captures: bibliographic information; study design; sample (N, age, sex, clinical status, recruitment source); manipulation (operationalization, label specificity, instructions); comparator; outcomes (modality, timing, instrument); effect size (d, g, r, η², F, t, M, SD, 95% CI as reported; computed from raw data where unreported); pre-registration status; funding source; conflicts of interest; risk of bias (RoB 2 / ROBINS-I).

The realized extracted dataset for the meta-analyzed studies is at [`meta-analysis/extracted_effect_sizes.csv`](https://github.com/mikhaeelatefrizk/affect-labeling-review/blob/main/meta-analysis/extracted_effect_sizes.csv); the realized risk-of-bias table is at [`supplementary/risk_of_bias.csv`](https://github.com/mikhaeelatefrizk/affect-labeling-review/blob/main/supplementary/risk_of_bias.csv).

**Deviation from protocol.** The pre-registered protocol called for two independent extractors. In execution, extraction was conducted by a single reviewer (see Section 19). This deviation is documented in the manuscript's *Limitations* section.

## 21. Risk of bias

**RoB 2** (Sterne et al., 2019) for randomized controlled trials, across five domains. **ROBINS-I** for non-randomized intervention studies. An adapted **NRSI tool** for within-subjects experimental designs, focusing on confounding (order effects, fatigue), measurement, and reporting. **GRADE** for certainty across the body of work for each outcome.

## 22. Synthesis methods

**Primary synthesis.** Random-effects meta-analysis using the DerSimonian-Laird estimator for τ². Effect sizes will be Hedges' g. Where studies report multiple effect sizes, all will be included with three-level modeling to account for within-study dependence; lab will be a clustering variable to address author non-independence (the UCLA Lieberman/Craske axis is highly represented).

**Heterogeneity.** Cochran's Q, I², τ², and the 95% prediction interval. Substantial heterogeneity (I² > 50%) will trigger pre-specified moderator analyses.

**Pre-specified moderators.** Lab affiliation (UCLA-axis vs. independent); stimulus valence; sample population; language of labeling; outcome timing; sample-size quartile.

**Publication bias.** Funnel-plot inspection, Egger's regression test, PET-PEESE, and p-curve. Multivariate funnel plots stratified by lab will also be presented.

**Sensitivity analyses.** Leave-one-out; restriction to RCTs only; restriction to studies with pre-registration; restriction to physiological outcomes; restriction to independent-lab studies; alternative τ² estimators (REML, Paule-Mandel).

**Software.** R packages `metafor` (Viechtbauer, 2010) and `meta` (Balduzzi et al., 2019); Python (`numpy`, `pandas`, `scipy`, `matplotlib`) for descriptive analyses and figure generation. All analysis code deposited on OSF and GitHub.

## 29. Additional information

This protocol explicitly addresses three structural concerns identified in the affect-labeling literature that have not been addressed by prior narrative reviews:

1. **Author non-independence.** Approximately 30 of the 50 most-cited affect labeling papers come from the UCLA Lieberman/Craske axis or close collaborators. The pre-specified lab-stratified moderator analysis treats this as a structural rather than incidental feature of the evidence base.

2. **Outcome heterogeneity.** Prior reviews have alternated between neural, psychophysiological, and self-report outcomes without clear hierarchy. This protocol pre-specifies psychophysiology as the primary quantitative outcome and treats neural and self-report outcomes as secondary, narrative-synthesized streams.

3. **Construct heterogeneity.** Affect labeling has been operationalized in at least eight distinct ways across the literature. The extraction template captures the operationalization explicitly, and moderator analyses test whether forced-choice vs. free vs. own-emotion paradigms produce systematically different effect sizes.
