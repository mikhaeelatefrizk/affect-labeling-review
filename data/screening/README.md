# `data/screening/` — the screening corpus

This directory contains everything related to which papers were retrieved, screened, and ultimately included in the review.

## Quick answers to the two most-asked questions

> **"What year filter was applied to the searches?"**
> **None.** Per PROSPERO §7, the search was "from inception through the date of the final search," with no date or language restrictions at the database level. The final search window was Q1–Q2 2026. Language exclusions were made later, at the eligibility stage. See [`data/searches/search_strategy.md`](../searches/search_strategy.md) for the full record.

> **"Where is the list of 100 included papers I can use to label my re-run corpus?"**
> A complete per-paper list does not exist. The PRISMA flow records 100 included papers at the synthesis stage, but the per-paper screening decisions were not preserved in a shareable form. What you can use:
> - [`included_papers.csv`](included_papers.csv) — the **22 of 100** confidently-identifiable includes, derived from the analysis code (meta-analysis input + risk-of-bias table). Treat as **gold-standard positives**.
> - [`all_references.csv`](all_references.csv) — every entry from `references.bib` parsed as a structured row, flagged for confirmed-include status, with a `manuscript_mentions` count column you can use as a soft inclusion signal (high counts ⇒ probably included).
> - [`derived_screening_log.csv`](derived_screening_log.csv) — the 3,892 PubMed re-derived candidates joined against the included list. This is the positive-unlabelled (PU) dataset.
>
> See the full discussion in [`docs/faq.md`](../../docs/faq.md) and the labeling strategies in [`docs/for-ml-researchers.md`](../../docs/for-ml-researchers.md).

## Why 22 and not 100

The published PRISMA flow records **100 included papers** at the synthesis stage, broken down as: 42 neuroimaging/neurostimulation studies, 9 in the quantitative meta-analysis of peripheral physiology (7 unique studies, 9 effect sizes), 28 behavioral/self-report studies, 12 clinical/patient-population studies, and 9 existing meta-analyses synthesized narratively (`prisma/prisma_counts.csv`).

`included_papers.csv` lists only the **22 of 100** that can be programmatically and unambiguously identified from the analysis code:
- The 7 studies in the random-effects meta-analysis input (from `meta-analysis/run_meta_analysis.py`).
- The 19 studies in the risk-of-bias summary (from `supplementary/build_rob_figure.py`).
- (4 of these overlap, hence 7 + 19 − 4 = 22 unique.)

The remaining 78 included studies are cited throughout the manuscript prose without a structured per-paper record. They exist in `references.bib`, but **the boundary between "included study" and "background citation" was not flagged per-entry** in the original screening, so we cannot reliably reconstruct it after the fact.

This is the load-bearing limitation of the public dataset and is acknowledged transparently:

- In the manuscript itself (Section 4.6, Limitations).
- In `extract_included_list.py` (docstring "Honest disclosure").
- In `docs/for-masters-students.md` (the onboarding letter for ML thesis work).
- In `docs/faq.md` (the FAQ).

## How to use this for labeling a re-run corpus

If you have re-run the pre-registered search across databases and want to label your merged corpus, the recommended workflow is:

1. **Strict positives (n = 22):** match rows of your corpus by DOI or fuzzy title against `included_papers.csv`. These are gold-standard positives.
2. **Soft / candidate positives:** match your corpus against `all_references.csv` rows where `is_confirmed_include = 0` AND `manuscript_mentions ≥ 2`. These are highly likely to be either additional included studies or topically-relevant background — both are useful signal for a screening classifier. Roughly half should be true positives; verify per-paper.
3. **Unlabeled:** everything in your corpus that doesn't match either CSV. Standard positive-unlabelled framing applies (≈2% hidden-positive contamination per `data/QUALITY_REPORT.md`).

For full ML-training strategies (PU classifiers, PubMedBERT fine-tuning, evaluation protocols), see [`docs/for-ml-researchers.md`](../../docs/for-ml-researchers.md).

## Important: what this dataset is and isn't

### What it is

A reproducible, schema-validated corpus of approximately 1,800 candidate papers retrieved from PubMed using the canonical pre-registered search query, each labeled with a binary `included_in_synthesis` flag. Suitable for training and evaluating AI-assisted SLR screening systems.

### What it is not

The original per-paper screening decisions made during the systematic review were not preserved in a shareable form. Specifically, the dataset *does not* contain:

1. **Per-paper exclusion reasons.** Aggregate counts for the 182 full-text exclusions (broken out into 6 reasons) are in `prisma/prisma_counts.csv`. The 1,289 title/abstract exclusions were not categorized per-paper.
2. **The title/abstract vs. full-text decision distinction.** In the derived dataset, `included_in_synthesis` collapses both stages into a single binary label.
3. **The exact original corpus.** PubMed is a living database; re-running the pre-registered query today will retrieve more papers than the original 1,842 (since publications continue to be indexed). The drift is reported in `data/QUALITY_REPORT.md`. The canonical 1,842 number from the published PRISMA flow remains authoritative for the manuscript.
4. **The full per-paper enumeration of the 100 included studies.** Only 22 are programmatically identifiable; the rest are cited in the manuscript prose without per-citation labels (see "Why 22 and not 100" above).

These limitations are common to retrospective derivation of SLR data. Please report them honestly when re-using the dataset.

## Schema

See [`screening_log.schema.json`](screening_log.schema.json) for the full JSON Schema. Required fields: `record_id`, `title`, `decision`. All other fields are optional. The schema accepts both:

- Canonical screening logs (with explicit per-paper decisions and exclusion reasons), and
- Derived screening logs (with only binary inclusion labels).

## Validation

```bash
python scripts/validate_screening_log.py
```

This will:
1. Schema-validate every screening log CSV against the JSON Schema.
2. Cross-check that the count of `included` rows matches the canonical 100 from `prisma_counts.csv` — but only for hand-labeled logs. `derived_screening_log.csv` is treated as positive-unlabelled (partial recall expected); `included_papers.csv` is documentation, not a screening log.
3. Cross-check identifier formats (PMID digits-only, DOI matches `10.x/y` pattern, year plausible).

Failures are emitted with row-level error messages.

## Methodological note

This review was screened by a single coder. Inter-rater agreement statistics are not available. If you re-screen the corpus with an independent coder, please publish the second screening pass as a separate, dated CSV (e.g., `screening_log__second_screen_2026.csv`) rather than overwriting the existing data. Cohen's κ between the two passes can then be computed reproducibly.
